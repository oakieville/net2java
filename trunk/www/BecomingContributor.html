Getting Involved in NET2Java

Danny Coward, January 5th 2007

This page is for those of you who want to do more than just use NET2Java technology. If you answer yes to one of the following questions...
  • I want to add my translations back to the NET2Java library !
  • I want to fix a bug in the NET2Java parser !
  • I want to add some test cases to NET2Java !
  • I want to add more sample applications rto NET2Java !
  • I want to add a feature to NET2Java !

...then read on. Here's what I need you to do:-

1) Let's talk ! Drop me a note: danny dot coward at sun dot com and let me know what you'd like to check back in, and give me a little background as to what you are using the technology for.

2) Let's share !  Next we need to make sure that you are ok with sharing the ideas you are contributing. So you need to fill out this form. I am not a lawyer, and if you are unsure about whether you or the company you work for is ok with sharing the IP rights of those ideas you may contribute, then you need to check with your own. Drop me a note and I'll ask you to FAX a signed copy to a number I'm not going to put on the internet :-)

3) House rules  If we get this far, there are some house rules for being a committer, they are simple. By being a committer you are agreeing to them, as follows.

  • If you do a checkin, you have to make sure all the tests continue to run.
  • If you add a feature or fix a bug, you must add a test case for it.
  • I'm all for those who do the work having a say in the future direction of the technology, but I have the final say on contraversial proposals or changes to the technology.
  • I might have to revoke your contributor rights. If you break the build, add features without tests, do anything against the goal of this technology ("Together we can translate every .NET application into Java !"), are unreasonable, unwilling to compromise, or are rude or unhelpful or obnoxious to any member of the NET2Java community, or any of the other irritating behaviors I've experienced in my working life, I'll probably do so.
  • Finally, these rules might change depending on how we all get along.
